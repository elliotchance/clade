Jingles

827 releases • 2 cataloged
Short, melodic phrases, traditionally used in advertisements, and later in ringtones and video games.
Read more Jingles are very short, melodic phrases of music. Jingles are often upbeat, catchy, and bright, but due to their close historic ties with soundtracking for screen or interactive media, jingles can be used to communicate a wide variety of moods and emotions. Traditionally, jingles were almost exclusively used for advertisements on television and radio, and typically lasted 15, 30, or 60 seconds long to fit to standard advertisement lengths. This same format was also seen in network-related television bumpers. Library Music was often composed for this express purpose, with it being commonplace for a composer to provide multiple different lengths and arrangements of one song for a variety of different time slots.

Jingles first began to grow shorter with the advent of musical stings, which are often only a few seconds long and are used as punctuation in soundtracking, such as to introduce a segment or show. Musical stings featured heavily in game shows, and sitcoms often to lighten the mood, and conversely in horror to accentuate scares. These stings are often used as a musical substitute for Sound Effects and are typically only a few seconds long. This style would develop into usage in ringtones (which looped these phrases) and other facets of consumer electronics as standins for operating system or video game sound effects. Some iconic jingles in this style include Windows XP Start Up, Nokia Tune 2003, and Open Treasure Box (宝箱) from ゼルダの伝説 時のオカリナ [The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time].
