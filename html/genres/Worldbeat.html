Worldbeat

662 releases • 2 cataloged
Pairs elements from diverse sources from across the globe with contemporary, uplifting Westernised production, created primarily for a Western audience.
Read more Worldbeat refers to an approach to music which mixes influences from diverse Regional Music from across the globe with contemporary production cemented in a mix of popular Western styles. It is also primarily created for a generalised Western audience, differentiating it from the many fusions of Pop and traditional music originally intended for a specific local market. It often crosses over with New Age and Chillout, and can be compared to the earlier Exotica trend. Worldbeat artists often pair this musical approach with socially conscious lyrics and a "world citizen" philosophy, aiming to break down cultural barriers. On the other hand, worldbeat has drawn criticism for exoticising, homogenising and watering down non-Western music, as well as for cultural appropriation due to the influences and samples used often being uncredited.

The term was coined in 1983 by the Austin, Texas-based Dan Del Santo and his friend Joe Nick Patowski, a writer for the Texas Monthly, to describe his eclectic musical style and conscious musical philosophy. It would then spread to southern California to describe similar acts there, such as Zulu Spear. The international music press would then pick the term up, but would for a while use it to also describe non-Western music. Throughout the 1980s, the genre's popularity would be boosted through the efforts of influential Rock artists such as Paul Simon with his album Graceland, which exhibits the eclectic, smooth approach of future worldbeat, and Peter Gabriel whose label Real World Records and festival WOMAD would highlight worldbeat artists alongside more standard international music. The marketing term "world music" was created by record executives in 1987, which would become the main term used for the latter purpose, restoring worldbeat to its original definition for most people.
