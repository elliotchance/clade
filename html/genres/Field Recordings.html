Field Recordings

AKA: Phonography • 8,013 releases
Material recorded outside of a recording studio for the purpose of capturing the audible illustration of an environment.
Read more Field recordings is a term denoting a set of practices regarding the recording of phenomena that occurs outside of a traditional musical setting. This term encompasses many sources, particularly natural and anthropogenic in nature. Releases in this field often have an external purpose to them, such as documenting Animal Sounds to educate on a given animal or animals, or Nature Recordings for relaxation or background noise.

Field recordings as a general practice are traceable back to the first instances of recorded sound, with celebrated wildlife recordist Ludwig Koch having started his career as a nature recordist as early as 1889. As advances in technology increased for sound recording, specifically with the phonograph, so too did the breadth of phonography, a term for field recordings intended to draw parallels between it and photography. As sound in visual media was developed and utilized, field recordists were employed for use in developing atmosphere within these media, such as former Cabaret Voltaire member Chris Watson recording for the television show Springwatch.

Luc Ferrari would utilize field recordings as a reaction to the edited nature of the Musique concrète genre he helped pioneer, with pieces like Presque rien nº 1 [Le lever du jour au bord de la mer] having a similar structure to that of musique concréte without losing the qualities of the unedited recordings. Field recordings have been utilized in a slew of genres revolving around atmosphere like Tribal Ambient and Mallsoft to draw attention to the geographical locations that both respective genres centered their aesthetics around.

Hierarchy

See all genres
    • Field Recordings
      • Nature Recordings

        Recordings of natural phenomena such as waterfalls, ocean waves, and storms, as well as Animal Sounds.

        • Animal Sounds

          Recordings which are done in order to capture sounds produced by animals.

          • Bird Sounds

            Recordings of birds, including vocalizations (songs and calls) and mechanical sounds produced by bills, wings, or feathers.

          • Insect Sounds

            Recordings that capture the sounds produced by insects.

          • Whale Song

            Low-frequency, complex vocalizations produced by baleen whales.

        • Rain Sounds

          Naturally occurring sounds of rainfall.

      • Radio Broadcast Recordings

        Recordings of transmissions sent by radio waves, used for sending communications to a wide audience.
