Slowed & Reverb

AKA: Slowed and Reverb Slowed + Reverb • 397 releases
Slowing down and adding reverb to pre-existing songs.
Read more Slowed & reverb is an internet remix genre that emerged on platforms like YouTube and SoundCloud in the late 2010s, consisting of slowing down and adding reverb to pre-existing songs from a wide range of genres. Slowed & reverb was prototyped by the similarly sample-based, slowed down Vaporwave and Chopped and Screwed genres, with slowed & reverb sometimes criticized as a "gentrified" version of the latter style. Slowed & reverb remixes are typically created through free, easy to use programs like Audacity, and are often uploaded with Anime visuals. These remixes often aim to evoke melancholy and/or nostalgia.

Slowed & reverb first saw popularity in 2017 with the virality of producer Jarylun Moore's slowed & reverb remix of Lil Uzi Vert's song "20 Min," with the genre growing in popularity during the COVID-19 pandemic. Slowed & reverb drew comparisons to the aesthetics and subdued nature of the similarly popular Lo-Fi Hip Hop genre. Due to slowed & reverb's popularity, some established artists like Ghost have released official slowed & reverb versions of their own music.
