Ambient

81,739 releases • 46 cataloged
Emphasizes texture and tone over traditional musical structure, aimed at evoking a particular atmosphere or mood.
Read more Ambient is a broad spectrum of music that puts more emphasis on the development of sound and texture than conventional musical structure, with the intention of evoking a particular atmosphere or mood. Ambient music can be composed from virtually any sound source, including both acoustic and electronic instruments, audio samples, vocals, found objects, and digital effects. Ambient music is typically instrumental, but some artists also utilize singing as well, generally as a component in creating a broader atmosphere rather than being the focal point of a piece.

The origins of ambient music are diverse, with early instances of the genre being influenced by instrumental music from other genres such as Jazz, Modern Classical (particularly Minimalism), New Age, early Electronic music, and various forms of Experimental music. Erik Satie’s series of five “Musique d’ameublement” compositions written between 1917-1923, and specifically intended to be played as unobtrusive background music during social gatherings, are said to be the first deliberate attempt at ambient music, with composer John Cage, another influential figure within ambient music, bringing a renewed interest to Satie’s work in the mid-20th century. Brian Eno also had a pivotal role in the establishment of the genre, being credited with coining the term “ambient music” in the liner notes of his 1978 album Ambient 1: Music for Airports, writing “Ambient Music must be able to accommodate many levels of listening attention without enforcing one in particular; it must be as ignorable as it is interesting.”

Since its inception, ambient music has influenced a number of subgenres, including Dark Ambient, a type of atmospheric Post-Industrial music; the synthesizer-driven Space Ambient, derived from Progressive Electronic and Berlin School; as well as Tribal Ambient and Ambient Americana, both of which incorporate traditional instrumentation from various regions. Although ambient music as a whole has seen scarce commercial success throughout its history, it maintains a dedicated audience due to its creative versatility, as well as its compatibility with other contemporary genres.

          Incorporates elements such as guitars, synthesizers, harsh or whispered vocals, raw production, or other elements associated with Black Metal and Dungeon Synth.

        • Ritual Ambient

          Dark, disturbing, ritualistic, and occult atmosphere, often taking inspiration from various existing ritual practices around the world.

      • Space Ambient

        Flowing and relaxing synthesizer-based music derived from Progressive Electronic, often oriented around imagery of outer space.

      • Tribal Ambient

        Combines the theory and atmosphere of Ambient with traditional instruments and sounds from around the globe.
