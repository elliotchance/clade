New Age

12,459 releases • 19 cataloged
Broad genre centered on peaceful, meditative melodies, using a wide variety of instrumentation.
Read more New age is a genre of music that tends to focus on peaceful, meditative melodies and is often intended to provide the listener with a sense of relaxation, meditation or optimism. The genre can take different forms: electronic new age uses soft synths, long, sustained notes and simple drum beats, while acoustic new age uses instruments such as flutes, piano and guitar to create a calm atmosphere (although sometimes even these acoustic instruments are digital counterparts). Mike Oldfield, Enigma, Popol Vuh, and Vangelis are some of the most prominent artists in the genre.

Some artists have even blended specific types of folk music into their own to create very distinct sounds, such as Enya and Clannad, who mixed new age and Celtic Folk Music to make Celtic New Age, and Cusco and Medwyn Goodall, whose South American influences stylized Andean New Age.

Hierarchy

See all genres
    • New Age
      • Andean New Age

        Takes heavy influence from Indigenous Andean Music, emphasizing a peaceful and harmonious atmosphere and often using the flute.

      • Celtic New Age

        Incorporates influences and performing styles from Celtic Folk Music, emphasizing pastoral imagery and wistful themes.

      • Native American New Age

        Takes heavy influence from Indigenous North American Music, emphasizing a peaceful and harmonious atmosphere and utilizing chants, drumming, and flutes.

      • Neoclassical New Age

        Strongly influenced by and sometimes also based upon early music and the Baroque Music or Classical Period.

      • New Age Kirtan

        American development of Kirtan, fusing it with New Age and Western music.

      • Tibetan New Age

        Incorporates perceived elements of Tibetan Music, particularly singing bowls.
